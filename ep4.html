Smart Trolley Using Arduino Nano

Creating a smart trolley using an Arduino Nano can revolutionize shopping experiences, adding convenience, efficiency, and even environmental benefits. Here's a basic guide on how to build one:

Components Needed:

Arduino Nano: To control the smart features and interface with sensors.
Sensors:
Weight Sensor: To detect items placed in the trolley.
RFID Reader: To identify items with RFID tags.
Ultrasonic Sensor: To detect obstacles and prevent collisions.
Display: To provide feedback and information to the user.
Actuators: (Optional) Such as motors for autonomous navigation.
Power Source: Battery or power bank to supply power to the Arduino and components.
Enclosure: To house the electronics and protect them from damage.
Optional: Bluetooth module for wireless communication with a smartphone app.
